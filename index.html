    Healthcare Data Mart with SCD0

    My Role

    20% as Corporate BI Architect
    80% as BI Technical responsible, Scrum Master and Development Team

    Previous Existing Solution

    No analytical system existed.
    All reporting came from operational databases.

    New Requirements

    Huge data integration problems due to the creation of analytical reporting and KPI from independent operational databases.
    Existing analytical and operational reporting had big performance and maintenance problems.
    Different corporate roles created different corporate reports and KPI with similar nomenclature but obtained using different criteria.
    Recent data (months) can be changed and no mark is set.
    Identified several (tents) of operational products to integrate.
    Creation of an integration process that could merge master data from different operational systems.
    Analytical solution should have an initial maximum delay of 7 days. NOTE: Afterwards a new requirement wanted a 24 hour delay maximum.
    Creation of a scalable solution.
    Creation of a low maintenance cost solution.
    Lowest operational impact possible.

    New Solution Characteristics

    Creation of a SCD0 Data Mart solution that integrates data from several operational products (silos).
    Scalable solution that continuously integrates new operational products (silos) quite fast (low cost).
    Low maintenance system.
    Minimum precedence exists.

    Previous Existing Solution

    We’ve created previously the first analytical system. See Healthcare Data Mart with SCD0 (presented before).

    New solution is scalable, simpler (much less precedencies between components) and has a lower maintenance cost.
    The solution takes control of all changes made in most important attributes of operational master data, even when operational system didn’t keep control of it.
    Creation of a new Semantic layer for Self-Service use with business nomenclature.

    In-house Registry Master Data Using Heuristic Rules

    My Role

    20% as Corporate BI Architect
    80% as BI Technical responsible, Scrum Master and Development Team

    Previous Existing Solution

    No MDM product existed neither an integrated alike product.
    Tens of operational products existed as silos (only with communication interfaces between products existed).
    Integrated data analysis was a big challenge.

    New Requirements

    Business users decided a MDM alike system was needed.
    There no big resources in order to create a new solutions.
    The system should infer (as much as possible) the master data mapping between operational products.
    For version 1 no specific human resource exists in order to validate and set master data mapping between operational systems.

    New Solution Characteristics

    An in-house MDM system registry type was created.
    For version 1 heuristic rules were applied in order to match as many codes and descriptions as possible between tens of master tables from operational products.
    Final solution and data mapping was defined by business users as ‘a huge step forward’ regarding data integration.
    This solution was the base for the first Data Mart of the company.

    In-house Registry and Centralized Master Data

    My Role

    Data Architect and Analyst

    Previous Existing Solution

    An in house MDM alike system existed but no analytical solution used it.
    Several limitations existed and an enormous list of new requirements were waiting to be developed.

    New Requirements

    A group of business users decided a new MDM system was needed mainly to standardize analytical data in the company.
    Business users should be able to manage their own data.
    MDM should integrate data from all company operational products (several existed).

    New Solution Characteristics

    A new in-house MDM solution was created.
    The system was non-intrusive, meaning no business master data process was changed (for iteration 1). Master data management done in an operational product remained been managed there.
    The new MDM was registry and centralized type.
    A GUI was created in order to the business users manage the centralized MDM data as required.
    The new MDM was the only source for the Conformed Dimension solution in Analytical products.
    MDM data was also published to every operational products and all interfaces regarding master data between operational products were eliminated improving security and maintenance costs.

    Corporate Self-Service

    My Role

    BI Architect, Analyst and Developer

    Previous Existing Solution

    Previous reporting tool wasn’t updated for several years and had several known limitations.
    Only centralized reports from IT department could be publish to the organization.
    Till then employees were able to:
    • change existing analytical reports (very limited) and save them in a personal repository
    • create excel files connected to existing DW/DM

    There wasn’t any self-service corporate publication solution.
    They could only share the reports/analysis in excel through email or file share.

    New Requirements

    A powerful corporate tools is needed for reporting with self-service functionality. New tool must be able to connect to existing and new Multidimensional, Tabular and Dimensional (RDBMS) models.
    Organization is open to purchase a new tool if justified.
    The new analytical corporate self-service solution should be created inside/part of the centralized corporate reporting solution.
    Publication inside each departments and areas is needed. Security segmentation is needed.
    For 90% of the use cases the solution should be completely autonomous. No IT tasks should exist, only business user’s tasks.

    New Solution Characteristics

    After evaluating corporate and market reporting solutions a new tool is chosen: PowerBI (Azure and On-Premise).
    For self-service creation and publication, new business processes where designed and the power user role was created.
    Several repositories where created inside PowerBI for self-service publication. Different corporate segmentations/analysis vectors where taken into account, such as:
    • departments
    • areas
    • product types
    • sales regions
    • …

    The new fast and agile solution empowered Business users and they’re now able to create and share new report and analysis using a corporate tool.
    Excel solution still exists and has now enhanced capacities. It’s completely integrated in the new processes.
    As wanted, for more than 90% of uses cases no IT task is needed, not even for security management.

    Integration of Clustering and Scoring Data in Data Mart

    Data Audit

    My Role

    20% as Corporate BI Architect
    80% as BI Technical responsible, Scrum Master and Development Team

    Previous Existing Solution

    No data audit existed.
    Several, mostly manual checks, where done periodically on operational products.
    The cost of these processes was very high: many people for a long time (periodically).
    There was no integration data auditing (operational products as silos).

    New Requirements

    Product owner doesn’t want data to be changed / corrected in analytical solutions.
    For every data problem or improvement a business process should exist in order to change that data on data sources (operational products).

    New Solution Characteristics

    Created and documented new business processes for data correction or improvement.
    A new audit product was created and is executed after the Analytical execution ends.
    Looking in all Analytical products repositories (Integration, Data Mart, MDM, .etc) the system is able to detect data to be improved and notifications are send following the business process defined.
    The system is easily managed, scalable and has no impact on the Analytical products execution.

    Centralized KPI Metadata and Repository

    My Role

    20% as Corporate BI Architect
    80% as BI Technical responsible, Scrum Master and Development Team

    Previous Existing Solution

    There’s no KPI corporate solution, meaning there’s no centralized product for KPI definition, execution, warehousing or documentation.
    There was no single source of truth for KPI’s, so different employees generated similar KPI and Dashboards using personal definition and formulas that don’t match between them.

    New Requirements

    In order to have a single source of the truth the company wants a centralized solution for KPI definition and formulas.
    The KPI results will be used in corporate reporting where historical data must exist (for tendencies).
    There’s a need to present the KPI results to some business user through self-service in order for them to use in their own reports.
    Due to the volume and complexity of some KPI the product owner wants then to be pre-calculated (if possible).

    New Solution Characteristics

    Created and documented new business processes for KPI definition and usage.
    Created a centralized KPI Metadata product for:
    • Business definition
    o Concepts definition
    o KPI definition
    • Technical definition
    o Business concepts – Data Mart attributes mapping
    o KPI calculation formula
    • Execution scheduler

    The KPI Metadata product has one business owner role (1) and several business users (N).
    Created a KPI execution system for daily pre-calculated KPI creation.
    Created a corporate KPI repository inside de Data Mart for saving KPI pre-calculated results. Historical data is maintained.
    Security implemented in all 3 modules using business roles defined on business processes.
